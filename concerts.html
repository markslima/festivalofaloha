Thursday, May 24th, 2012, 7:30pm

Opening Ceremony and Concert

Aloha Lives!

Drums of Tahiti

A Pacific Soundz production

Maurer Concert Hall, 4544 N. Lincoln Ave., East Bldg

$25 General Public / $23 Old Town School Members / $21 Seniors & Children

The Festival of Aloha officially opens with a sacred ceremony including traditional `oli (chanting) and hula (dance) presented by Ke Kula Kupa`a O Ka Pakipika under the direction of Kumu Hula Lanialoha Lee. Lanialoha is joined by elders from the American Indian Center, Kumu Hula Puanani Edgar, and Alaka`i Nani Edgar. Also performing are Halau I Ka Pono, the Hula School of Chicago, under the direction of Kumu Hula June Tanoi and students of `Ohana Entertainment.

Drums of Tahiti brings the far reaches of the South Pacific to the Midwest. Produced by Lanialoha Lee, and featuring Terii Humuhai’s Tahitian drumming with Moeata Tehani’s `Ori Tahiti. Together this trio and cast members join to relive the ways of Tahiti.

Aloha Lives! is the newest production by Pacific Soundz featuring Lanialoha Lee. The voice of Lanialoha, her signature power strums for the `ukulele and her band, Aloha Lives!, perform a seamless and memorable concert of melodies, harmonies and rhythmic sounds that will inspire you

Friday, May 25th, 2012 8:30pm

Let's Kanikapila kakou!

Hosted by Daniel Ho, Darlene Ahuna, and Kumu Hula Puanani Sylvia Edgar & Alaka`i Nani Edgar

Szold Dance and Music Hall, 4545 N. Lincoln Ave., West Building

All tickets $10

Don’t miss this fun Hawaiian-style jam and dance party. Experience the cultural practice of Kanikapila that exemplifies the Hawaiian ideal of sharing. A hula lesson will be taught by Kumu Hula Puanani Edgar and accompanied by Daniel Ho. Bring your `uke, slack key guitar or Hawaiian steel guitar and get ready to hula or sing along. So much like the islands you’ll swear you’re sitting in Tutu’s carport. Aloha!

Saturday, May 26th, 2012 8:00pm

Daniel Ho with guest Jason Arimoto

4544 N. Lincoln Ave., East Building

$25 General Public / $23 Old Town School Members / $21 Seniors & Children

Daniel performs original songs in English and Hawaiian that feature his versatility on `ukulele, slack key guitar and vocals. As an independent record company owner, he specializes in Hawaiian and acoustic-themed music. Daniel continues to pursue excellence in composition, performance, audio engineering and production.

Daniel with be joined by Jason Arimoto on `ukulele. The Honolulu Star-Advertiser has described Jason’s style as “what sounds on first take like an electric guitar is Arimoto playing `ukulele through various effects. Guitarists have been experimenting with the sonic possibilities of their electrified instrument for more than 60 years, and Arimoto is picking up where Jake Shimabukuro left off.”

Any Festival of Aloha patron who attends one of the three Hula `Auana workshops offered by Kumu Hula Puanani Edgar will be invited up on stage to perform the Hula they learned accompanied by Daniel. It’s a not-to-be-missed opportunity for hula dancers.

Sunday, May 27th, 2012 7:00pm

Darlene Ahuna with Daniel Ho

Maurer Concert Hall, 4544 N. Lincoln Ave., East Building

$20 General Public / $18 Old Town School Members / $16 Seniors & Children

Darlene Ahuna is truly one of the most esteemed ambassadors of Hawaiian music. She is a multiple Na Hoku Hanohano Award-winning vocalist, best known for her seamless falsetto renditions of traditional Hawaiian, hula and hapa-haole standards. She conveys a moving and mesmerizing style that is all her own, influenced by the late-greats, who include Lena Machado and Genoa Keawe. She is a mainstay performer at the Merrie Monarch Festivals, has represented the Big Island of Hawai’i at venerable Hawaiian events in Japan, and has travelled across the United States and enchanted audiences with her uplifting personality and genuine sense of aloha.
