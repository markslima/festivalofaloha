Discovering & Exploring the `Ukulele

Presenter: Daniel Ho

Recommended for Beginning and Intermediate levels of playing

FRIDAY, MAY 25, 11:00AM-1:00PM / SATURDAY, MAY 26, 11:00AM-1:00PM / SUNDAY, MAY 27, 9:00-10:30AM

Discovering the `Ukulele is a Beginner’s Guide to Technique, Tuning, Scales, Chords and Songs. Multiple Grammy award-winning musician and author Daniel Ho will give a workshop based on the `ukulele instructional book that he co-authored with Herb Ohta Jr. Discovering the `Ukulele is a product of their cumulative experiences as recording artists, performers, and teachers. Students of all skill levels are encouraged to bring their instruments. Daniel will also 'raise the bar’ as he guides workshop patrons into the more intermediate Guide to Strumming, Arpeggios, Chords, 3rd’s, 6th’s, Pentatonic Scales, Picking, Fingering, Turnarounds, and Songs. Presented here in Exploring the `Ukulele is a collection of some of the most requested subject matter, harvested from years of teaching thousands of students all over the world.

Writing Songs for `Ukulele and Other Instruments

Presenter: Daniel Ho

Recommended for All levels

FRIDAY, May 25, 1:30-3:00pm / SUNDAY, MAY 27, 2:30-4:00PM

This workshop is great knowledge for the many `ukulele players who are stepping out into the arena of performance. Knowledge that is applicable to any instrumentalist. Daniel will share his greatest passion and career secrets that have earned him multiple Grammys and is closest to his heart! Learn how to apply concepts of writing music to your instrument in terms of writing music. Three basic techniques that will be covered are developing melodies using themes, harmonizing melodies with chords, and creating `ukulele accompaniments using strumming and arpeggios. This workshop will open new doors for our `ukulele players at large.


Colorful Sounds

Presenter: Daniel Ho

Recommended for Intermediate and Advanced levels

SUNDAY, MAY 27, 11:00AM-1:00PM

Creative Harmony Made Simple. This is a more practical way of thinking about harmonic structure. Daniel Ho manages to “re-imagine tools” made easier for the student to grasp and apply. “This method focuses on module’s (group of notes) with uniform intervallic relationships and how they are used to build more complex harmonies.” Colorful Sounds is the result of 20 years of writing compositions, arrangements and performances. It is a ‘Harmonic Method’ that offers a “unique perspective” in one’s own musical creations. Students need a fundamental understanding of music theory to benefit from this material.

Recommended for Beginning level and Seasoned players of Slack Key Guitar

FRIDAY, MAY 25, 6:00-7:30PM

The G Kilauea Tuning, A New Musical Voice

Presenter: Daniel Ho

Recommended for Intermediate and Advanced Slack Key Guitar players

SATURDAY, MAY 26, 9:00-10:30AM

“It was only a decade ago and I remember it vaguely. I was on a month-long U.S. tour playing keyboards in the pit orchestra of a theatrical show. It was a circus, both literally and figuratively. As an artist, I was transitioning from one genre to another – contemporary jazz to Hawaiian – from keyboards to guitar. I was balancing my personal and financial responsibilities against my elusive quest for a new musical voice. With my travel guitar in hand, I searched and searched-on the tour bus, waiting at the airport, in the hotel room, at sound check. I can’t remember if it was on the 10-hour bus ride to Schenectady, or in a Cincinnati hotel room when it came to me, but I am ever so grateful it did. In recognition of the contemporary jazz band that I toured and recorded with for the prior six years, I named my discovery the G Kilauea tuning.

I have been using this tuning since 1998 for almost all my fingerstyle guitar playing. I have experimented with other tunings, but found the G Kilauea tuning to be the most versatile and functional for solo instrumentalist and vocal accompaniment.”

Tahitian Basics & Technique for `Ori [Phase I]

Presenter: `Orometua Moeata Tehani, accompanied by Terii Tumuhai

Recommended Level: All Levels. Bring `I`I’s.

THURSDAY, MAY 24, 2:30-4:00PM / FRIDAY, MAY 25, 1:30-3:00PM

This introductory workshop offers the basics broken down, and the proper technique required to build a solid foundation for `Ori Tahiti. The building blocks from stance and posture, positioning, hand, hip, and foot movements. Weather you are actively performing or interested in learning more about Tahitian culture and dance forms, this is the first stop of a three phase workshop series. This is an 80 minute workshop.

Tahitian Ote`a for `Ori [Phase II]

Presenter: `Orometua Moeata Tehani

Recommended Level: All Levels. Bring `I`I’s.

FRIDAY, MAY 25, 3:00-4:30PM

Workshop patrons are welcome to continue to grow their knowledge of Tahitian dance styles while further developing their basics and technique of `ori. Increase your listening abilities and recognition of Tahitian api [drum beats]. Build upon the foundation delivered in Tahitian Basics & Technique for `Ori while learning traditional ote`a hand movements and formations. This is an 80 minute workshop followed by a 10 minute break before combining with Tahitian Drumming-Basics & Technique for To`ere [Phase II].

Tahitian Drumming- Basics & Technique for To`ere [Phase II]

Presenter: `Orometua Moeata Tehani

Recommended Level: All Levels. Bring your own to`ere.

FRIDAY, MAY 25, 3:00-4:30PM

Welcome to Tahitian drumming. Learn the fundamental basics of to`ere drumming. This workshop begins with properly holding both to`ere and ra`au tairi (stick), and discovering the tones, pitches, and sounds of the to`ere. A variety of Otea Api (beats) from easy to difficult paces, solo & ote`a will be introduced, and their respective traditional phrasing. This is an 80 minute workshop followed by a 10 minute break before combining with Tahitian Ote`a for `Ori [Phase II].

Tahitian Hivinau for `Ori [Phase III]

Presenter: `Orometua Moeata Tehani

Recommended Level: Intermediate & Advanced `Ori. Bring I`I’s.

FRIDAY, MAY 25, 4:30-6:00PM / SATURDAY, MAY 26, 11:00AM-1:00PM

Workshop patrons will be challenged to increase their level of proficiency. Experience more rigorous and complicated hip movements. The dance formations are Hivinau focused traditional formations, including traditional chant styles characteristic of Hivinau. This is an 80 minute workshop followed by a 10 minute break before combining with Tahitian Drumming-Hivinau for To`ere [Phase III].

Tahitian Drumming-Hivinau for To`ere [Phase III]

Presenter: `Orometua Moeata Tehani

Recommended Level: Intermediate & Advanced To`ere. Bring your own to`ere.

FRIDAY, MAY 25, 4:30-6:00PM / SATURDAY, MAY 26, 11:00AM-1:00PM

Welcome to Tahitian drumming for active players of Tahitian culture. The fundamental basics of to`ere drumming are reinforced, while pahu and fa`atere are introduced. Api (beats) from easy, moderate, and difficult paces, solo beats, and their respective traditional phrasing will be realized. Traditional format of presenting Hivinau will be the focus and including the traditional Tahitian style chants characteristic of Hivinau will be taught. This is an 80 minute workshop followed by a 10 minute break before combining with Tahitian Hivinau for `Ori [Phase III].
