offered through the Old Town School of Folk Music

The Old Town School is home to the only Pacific Islands culture and arts program of its kind in the United States, led by instructor, performing artist and Kumu Hula Lanialoha Lee. Since 1997, Lee has built a program of more than 100 students each eight-week session, crossing education and instrument genres and departments, and ranging in ages from infants to adults. Lee has also worked closely with our performance and community outreach departments to ensure that nationally and internationally touring performers and educators from all Pacific Island cultures are represented in our seasons of field trips, concerts, and events.


Kanikapila

Truly embodying the “teach and celebrate” mission of the Old Town School, Kanikapila is a party, a gathering, and a jam session with music, dance and Pacific Island style food in a potluck style. Each session of classes culminates in this celebration, and we hold other Kanikapilas as we can throughout the year, including as a part of the Chicago Folk and Roots Festival.

Private Lessons

The Old Town School hosts approximately 20 students per week in private lessons for Ukulele and Tahitian drumming with Lanialoha Lee, affording her the ability to tailor genre preferences and learning style to each student's needs.
